NJV School Karachi

Founded in 1855, NJV School is the oldest public educational institute in Sindh. Today, NJV is a co-educational primary and secondary institute. It is a public school managed by the Akhuwat Foundation as part of the Sindh Government’s public-private partnership programme to help impro.ve public education systems. Located in the heart of Karachi on M.A. Jinnah Road, NJV School’s 150 year old campus has been declared a national heritage site. The school is currently undergoing a major overhaul in both infrastructure and academics

In 1910, another high school was established in Sindh, accommodating 250 students and receiving a monthly grant of 4,020 rupees. On November 16, 1987, the Department of Education, Government of Sindh, received a letter containing recommendations for improving the educational facilities at the NJV Government Higher Secondary School (Sindhi Medium) Karachi. In 1945-46, the school briefly relocated to the Kotwaal building before moving to the former Sindh Assembly building. The letter highlighted its historical role in Sindh’s education and the prominent figures it has produced, while noting its decline due to encroachments. It proposed relocating government offices to restore the school’s former glory and expand its capacity to over 300 students.
